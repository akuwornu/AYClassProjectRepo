Psychological Trauma-Theory, Research, Practice and Policy, a peer-reviewed journal from the American
Psychological Association that says it features psychological effects of trauma, published research articles on the
following topics in its July 2024 edition (Vol. 16, Issue 5):

Trauma-Related Shame:

* Does change in trauma-related shame predict change in PTSD symptomatology?

* Sentenced to shame: Moral injury exposure in former lifers.

Trauma Coping and Posttraumatic Growth:

* Associations between posttraumatic stress symptoms and posttraumatic growth elements: A network analysis.

* Family resilience in a crisis: A qualitative study of family resilience in multimember infected families in the context
of COVID-19.

* The relation between peritraumatic dissociation and coping strategies: A network analysis.

* Interacting with a friend after a trauma film reduces anxiety and intrusive memories.

Trauma Assessment:

* A systematic review evaluating PTSD treatment effects on intermediate phenotypes of PTSD.

* Assessing prevalence, validity, and correlates of ICD-11 posttraumatic stress disorder and complex posttraumatic
stress disorder in Ireland.
