IFLA, a peer-reviewed journal that says it features library and information services and the social, political and
economic issues that impact access to information through libraries from the International Federation of Library
Associations and Institutions, published research articles on the following topics in its June 2024 quarterly edition
(Vol. 50, Issue 2):

Original Articles:

* AI-generated content tools and students' critical thinking: Insights from a Chinese university

* Librarians' role in the preservation and dissemination of indigenous knowledge

* Professional qualifications, accreditation, and certification in library and information science schools: A global
perspective

* Impact of multimedia on academic information literacy instruction in libraries

* Knowledge management for climate change in South Africa: A proposed strategy

* Exploring the research domains, gender gap and labour market perspectives of doctoral research in library and
information science in India

* When a disaster strikes: Are libraries in the Philippines ready?

* Research data management in selected East African libraries: A survey

* Information competency assessment of undergraduates: A Pakistani perspective

* Redefining academic library work: Telecommuting potential in post-COVID Ghana
